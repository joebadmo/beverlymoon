  • Beginning

    July 20, 2011

    It’s been almost a year since the idea first popped into my head and more than a month since I actually took the initiative to get started on this project. Today, I brought Joe along to the place where the cases will be manufactured.

    There were some numbers and a few technical details we needed to hash out before we could get this rolling. I was excited when Stuart, my point of contact at Small Parts, took me on the tour the last time I visited, so I was glad when he offered to show Joe around. It’s nice to see the machines, the workers, and the space behind everything and I wanted to share that experience with him. The sound of drilling mixed with the smell of oil and the mass of the machines… all of it is just so raw and physical. And it’s surprisingly calm in there.

    I ordered the materials to make 50 sets tonight and they should arrive next week. So if all goes well… we might start the first production by the end of next week. Wow.

  • Corian Samples

    July 21, 2011

    I had a hard time trying to find some Corian sheets for the first run of sample production. Lots of headaches talking to various local shops and big box retailers.

    I finally got in touch with a rep and he graciously opened his own home to let me rummage through his piles of samples. So excited to see so many colors and options. I’ve been looking at the material online for the past few months and to actually feel the samples and see the colors was really helpful.

  • Colors

    July 22, 2011

    Picking out colors for the first sample production was not that easy since there were so many choices and I was limited to only 2 or 3 colors. Joe and I took some samples to our bathroom and looked at neutral colors that might work well in most homes.

    I decided that “Natural Grey” and “Glacier Ice” were the two colors that could really look beautiful on a bathroom sink. Super excited and nervous about the color choices!

  • First Samples

    September 01, 2011

    First 45 samples were ready to be picked up. Talked to Stuart, a SPM employee who’s been more than helpful along this process, about the troubles that they’ve been having with the lid and how the taper is causing the material to kick up and crack.

    I thought that this whole process was almost over but it seems like there might be more revisions than I anticipated. Good thing is that I have a few to give away and feel out how users are liking it.
